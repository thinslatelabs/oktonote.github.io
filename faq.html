Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about OktoNote's AI-powered features and intelligent note-taking capabilities.

General Features

What is OktoNote and how does it work?

OktoNote is an AI-powered note-taking app that transforms any input into organized knowledge cards. You can capture content through text, voice recordings, photos, or document uploads, and our AI automatically categorizes everything into 10 different card types: Tasks, Todos, Journals, Itineraries, Links, Attachments, Lists, Discussions, Details, and Study materials.

The app processes your input in real-time, extracting key information and organizing it intelligently so you can focus on capturing ideas rather than organizing them manually.

What are the 10 knowledge card types and how are they different?

OktoNote automatically categorizes your content into 10 specific card types:

  • Task Cards: General reminders without specific deadlines
  • Todo Cards: Time-sensitive tasks with specific deadlines
  • Journal Cards: Personal memories and experiences
  • Itinerary Cards: Schedules, travel plans, and events
  • Link Cards: Web URLs and website bookmarks
  • Attachment Cards: Files, documents, and media
  • List Cards: Organized collections and checklists
  • Discussion Cards: Meeting notes and collaborative conversations
  • Details Cards: Important information and quick facts
  • Study Cards: Learning materials and knowledge building

The AI analyzes your content's context and automatically assigns the most appropriate category.

How accurate is the AI categorization?

OktoNote's AI categorization is highly accurate because it analyzes context, keywords, and content structure. The system understands the difference between actionable tasks, time-sensitive todos, personal reflections, and informational content.

The AI continuously learns and improves its categorization based on content patterns, ensuring that your notes are organized in the most logical and useful way possible.

Input Methods & Capture

What input methods does OktoNote support?

OktoNote supports multi-modal input to capture information however is most convenient for you:

  • Text Input: Type directly with rich text support and auto-save
  • Voice Recording: Record voice memos with automatic transcription
  • Photo Capture: Snap photos with OCR text extraction and image enhancement
  • Document Upload: Upload PDFs, Word documents, spreadsheets, and other file formats

All input methods work seamlessly together, and the AI processes everything into organized knowledge cards.

How does voice recording and transcription work?

OktoNote's voice recording feature captures high-quality audio and automatically transcribes it to text. The transcription is processed in real-time, and you can play back the original audio anytime.

The AI doesn't just transcribe – it understands the content and extracts key insights, action items, and important details, then organizes them into the appropriate knowledge card categories.

What types of text can the photo OCR feature extract?

OktoNote's OCR technology can extract text from various sources including:

  • Handwritten notes and journals
  • Printed documents and books
  • Whiteboard content and presentations
  • Business cards and receipts
  • Signs, labels, and product information
  • Screenshots of digital content

The system enhances image quality automatically and extracts text accurately, even from challenging lighting conditions or curved surfaces.

What document formats can I upload and how are they processed?

OktoNote supports multiple document formats including PDF, Word documents (.docx), spreadsheets, text files, and more. When you upload documents:

  • Content is automatically parsed and extracted
  • Key information is identified and highlighted
  • Text becomes searchable across your entire knowledge base
  • Documents are stored with original formatting preserved

Large documents are intelligently summarized while maintaining access to the full original content.

Views & Organization

What's the difference between Feed View and Timeline View?

Feed View displays your AI-processed knowledge cards in an organized, categorized interface. Each card shows the processed information with clear categorization, key details, and visual indicators. You can filter by type, search across categories, and access information in a structured format.

Timeline View shows your content in chronological order exactly as you captured it. This view preserves your original input including raw voice recordings, original photos, unprocessed text, and uploaded documents. It's perfect for reviewing your thought process or accessing complete context.

Can I manually change how content is categorized?

While OktoNote's AI categorization is highly accurate, you maintain control over your content organization. You can review and adjust categorizations as needed, ensuring your knowledge cards are organized exactly how you prefer.

The system learns from your preferences over time, improving its automatic categorization to better match your personal organizational style.

How does filtering and sorting work in the Feed View?

Feed View offers powerful filtering and sorting options:

  • Filter by any of the 10 card types to focus on specific content
  • Sort by creation date, modification date, or relevance
  • Use category indicators to quickly identify content types
  • Apply multiple filters simultaneously for precise content discovery

The interface maintains your filter preferences, making it easy to return to your preferred view configurations.

Search & Discovery

How does OktoNote's intelligent search work?

OktoNote uses semantic search that understands context and meaning, not just exact keyword matches. You can search for concepts, feelings, or related topics and find relevant content even when the exact words don't appear in your notes.

The search works across all content types – text, transcribed audio, OCR'd images, and parsed documents. It understands relationships between ideas and can surface relevant information based on context and intent.

Can I search through voice recordings and images?

Yes! OktoNote makes all content searchable:

  • Voice recordings: Automatically transcribed text is fully searchable
  • Images: OCR-extracted text from photos is indexed and searchable
  • Documents: All parsed document content is searchable
  • Mixed content: Search across all formats simultaneously

This means you can find information regardless of how you originally captured it.

Does search work offline?

Yes, OktoNote's intelligent search works completely offline. Once your content has been processed by our AI services, the search index is stored on your device so you can search through your entire knowledge base even without an internet connection.

This offline-first approach ensures your information is always accessible and your searches are instant, regardless of connectivity.

How OktoNote Compares

How does OktoNote compare to Mem.ai?

OktoNote and Mem.ai serve different needs:

  • OktoNote: Focuses on daily life organization with multi-modal input (voice, photos, documents). Structured categorization into 10 card types makes it ideal for personal productivity and quick capture.
  • Mem.ai: Designed for professional knowledge work with flexible AI-driven collections. Better for research, team collaboration, and building extensive knowledge bases.

Choose OktoNote if: You want effortless capture from any source (voice, photos, documents) with automatic organization for daily life and work. Choose Mem.ai if you need advanced knowledge building and research capabilities.

How does OktoNote differ from Notion?

OktoNote and Notion have fundamentally different approaches:

  • OktoNote: AI-first capture and organization. Drop in any content type and AI automatically categorizes it. Optimized for quick capture and retrieval with minimal setup.
  • Notion: Manual structure creation with databases, templates, and blocks. Powerful for building custom workflows but requires significant setup and maintenance.

Choose OktoNote if: You want automatic organization without setup overhead. Choose Notion if you prefer building custom structures and don't mind the complexity.

How does OktoNote compare to Obsidian?

OktoNote and Obsidian target different use cases:

  • OktoNote: Automatic AI categorization with multi-modal input. Perfect for capturing diverse content types (voice, photos, documents) with zero manual linking or tagging required.
  • Obsidian: Manual knowledge graph building with markdown files. Excellent for researchers and note-takers who want to manually create connections between ideas.

Choose OktoNote if: You want effortless capture with automatic organization. Choose Obsidian if you enjoy manually building knowledge networks and prefer markdown-based workflows.
